Who She Is

About Shantel

Shantel is a dedicated educator who brings Home Science and Biology to life with warmth, depth, and an infectious love for learning. A graduate of the University of Eldoret with a BSc in Home Science, she understands that knowledge is most powerful when it connects directly to how we live — what we eat, how we care for ourselves and our families, and how our bodies work.

Beyond the classroom, Shantel is a curious soul on a constant journey of growth. Whether she's mastering a new recipe, perfecting her netball game, or diving into a subject she hasn't taught before, her belief is simple: a teacher who never stops learning is a teacher who never stops inspiring.

Home Science — Her Heart Subject

Home Science is more than a subject to Shantel — it's a philosophy of living well. From nutrition and textiles to family resource management and child development, she teaches her students that the science of everyday life is just as rigorous and rewarding as any laboratory discipline.

Her lessons are grounded in practical application: cooking demonstrations, nutritional analysis, environmental health, and consumer science all feature prominently. She believes when students see how a subject touches their daily lives, learning becomes genuinely exciting.

Biology — Decoding Living Systems

Teaching Biology allows Shantel to explore one of her deepest curiosities: how living things work. From cell biology to ecology, genetics to human physiology, she brings the complexity of life into sharp, accessible focus for her students.

She is committed to expanding her Biology knowledge every day — reading current research, following scientific developments, and translating new discoveries into classroom-ready content that sparks genuine wonder in her learners.

Cooking — Where Passion Meets Purpose

Shantel loves cooking — not just as a hobby, but as a daily practice of creativity, nourishment, and discovery. Inspired deeply by her Home Science background, she approaches the kitchen the way she approaches teaching: with curiosity, care, and a readiness to try something new.

Every new recipe is a new skill. Every technique mastered is another chapter in a lifelong education that no classroom can fully contain. Food, to Shantel, is connection — to culture, to family, and to the science of how our bodies are nourished.

Trend 01

Competency-Based Learning Over Content Delivery

CBC shifts the focus from what students know to what they can do. For Home Science, this means assessing practical skills — cooking labs, textile projects, nutrition analysis — alongside theory. For Biology, scientific inquiry, fieldwork, and lab investigations are now core assessment components rather than optional extras.

Home Science · Biology
Trend 02

Continuous Assessment Replaces High-Stakes Exams

Classroom-Based Assessment for Learning Activities (CALA) replaces end-of-year examinations with ongoing portfolio-based assessment. Home Science teachers document practical projects and cooking demonstrations; Biology teachers record lab reports, field studies, and scientific investigations throughout the year.

Assessment Reform
Trend 03

Learner-Centred Pedagogy as the New Standard

Teachers are now facilitators of discovery rather than lecturers of content. Students learn through project-based activities, peer collaboration, and real-world problem-solving. Home Science students plan and execute meals and household budgets; Biology students design experiments and analyse ecosystems in their local environments.

Pedagogy
Trend 04

Digital Literacy Integration Across All Subjects

CBC mandates ICT integration across all subjects. Home Science teachers are expected to use nutrition databases, recipe planning applications, and textile design software. Biology teachers leverage digital microscopes, virtual laboratory platforms, and online scientific databases to enrich practical learning experiences.

Digital Education
Trend 05

Life Skills and Holistic Education at the Core

CBC explicitly links academic content to real-life application. Home Science is recognised as a life skills subject — directly addressing nutrition, health, and family management in ways that are immediately applicable. Biology connects to health education, environmental conservation, and career pathways in medicine, agriculture, and environmental science.

Life Skills · Holistic
Trend 06

Environmental Sustainability in Science Teaching

CBC places strong emphasis on environmental stewardship and sustainability. Biology teachers are expected to incorporate ecology, conservation, and climate change into their lessons, connecting Kenya's rich biodiversity to global environmental challenges. Home Science teachers address sustainable food systems, waste management, and eco-friendly household practices aligned with Kenya's Vision 2030 green economy goals.

Sustainability · Biology

University of Eldoret — Shantel's Alma Mater

The University of Eldoret, located in Eldoret, Uasin Gishu County, Kenya, is one of the country's leading public universities. Established in 1994, it is renowned for its programmes in science, technology, education, and agriculture. The School of Education at UoE has produced thousands of dedicated teachers who serve across Kenya's education system.

Shantel's BSc in Home Science from UoE gave her the scientific rigour, practical skills, and educational foundation that defines her teaching today. The university's emphasis on applied knowledge and community service aligns perfectly with the values she brings to every classroom.
